Editing Tutorial

Editing your episodes

Light editing is all it takes. Your storyteller's authentic voice is the product — not polish.

Editing a ForeverSaid podcast is simpler than you think. You're not trying to make a professional radio show. You're trimming the dead air, adding intro/outro music, and exporting clean files. That's it. The real moments — the pauses, the laughter, the stumbles — stay in.

Choose your editing software

All three options below can handle everything you need. Pick whichever matches your comfort level.

📺 Bonus: Turn your podcast into a video for YouTube (click to expand)

Want to share your podcast via YouTube instead of (or in addition to) a podcast app? Many families prefer this — it's familiar, free, and easy to share a link. Here's how:

1. Create cover art. Use Canva (free) to design a simple image — your storyteller's name, a family photo, the ForeverSaid feel. This will be the visual that plays during the entire episode.

2. Combine audio + image. Use iMovie (free on Mac), Adobe Premiere, or even YouTube Studio to combine your audio file with the cover art image. The result is a "video" that shows your cover art while the audio plays.

3. Upload to YouTube. Upload each episode, create a playlist for the series, and set the videos to Unlisted — this means only people with the link can watch. It won't appear in search or on your channel publicly.

4. Share the playlist link. Send the playlist URL to your family. Done.

For the creative and ambitious: you can build photo slideshows or family images into each episode to create a visual experience. This is above and beyond — but if you've got the skills, go for it.

The editing process (5 steps)

Step 1: Import your audio

Open your editing software and import the audio file for one episode. In GarageBand, go to File > Open and select your recording. In Audacity, go to File > Import > Audio.

Step 2: Trim the rough spots

Listen through and remove only the obvious problems: long stretches of dead silence (more than 5 seconds), a section where someone answered a phone, or a moment where the storyteller explicitly asked to start over. Leave the natural pauses, the "ums," and the thinking moments — these are authentic and human. Select the section you want to remove, then press Delete.

Step 3: Add intro & outro music

Import your chosen intro track (from the 3 tracks included in your kit). Place it at the very beginning of the timeline. Import the outro track and place it at the very end, after the last words. Adjust the volume of the music so it's clearly softer than the speaking voice — the music should support, not compete. In GarageBand, drag the volume slider on the music track down to about 30-40%.

Step 4: Level the audio

If some parts are much louder or quieter than others, you can normalize the audio. In Audacity: select all (Ctrl+A), then go to Effect > Normalize and click OK. In GarageBand, the built-in compressor and leveling handles this automatically. This step is optional but makes the listening experience smoother.

Step 5: Export your finished episode

Export as MP3 or M4A. In GarageBand: Share > Export Song to Disk > MP3. In Audacity: File > Export > Export as MP3. Name the file clearly: "Episode-1-Growing-Up.mp3". Repeat for all 5 episodes.

Publishing your podcast

Once your episodes are exported, you need a place to host them so your family can listen. We recommend Spotify for Podcasters (formerly Anchor) — it's completely free, gives you a private link, and works in any podcast app or web browser.

Quick setup: Create a free account at podcasters.spotify.com, upload your episodes in order, set the podcast to "private" so only people with the link can find it, and share the link with your family. Takes about 10 minutes.

💡 The golden rule of editing

When in doubt, leave it in. The moments you're tempted to cut — the pauses, the searching for words, the emotional breaks — are often the moments your family will treasure most. You're preserving a person, not producing a performance.
